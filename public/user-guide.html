User Roles & Permissions

Who can do what in the system

Every staff member is given a role when their account is created. The role decides what they can see and do in the system.

👑 Owner
  • Full access to everything
  • Can manage all staff accounts
  • Can change company settings
  • Can view all financial reports
  • Only one person should be Owner
🛡 Admin
  • Same as Owner, except:
  • Cannot create other Admins
  • Cannot change company settings
  • Can manage Cashiers and Salesmen
🧾 Cashier
  • Can create sales invoices
  • Can receive customer payments
  • Can view inventory
  • Cannot change prices or settings
  • Cannot delete records
🚗 Salesman
  • Can create sales invoices
  • Can view customers
  • Cannot access accounts
  • Cannot see financial reports
  • Field sales staff role
💡 Best Practice

Give Cashier role to counter staff, Salesman role to field reps, Admin to your manager, and keep Owner for yourself only.

Module 1

Inventory Management

Track your products, batches, and stock levels

The Inventory module is where all your medicines and products are stored. Every product has batches — each batch has its own expiry date and purchase price, so the system always knows which stock to sell first (oldest expiry first).

Adding a New Product

1
Click "Inventory" in the left menu.
2
Click the "Add Product" button (top right corner).
3
Fill in the product details:
  • Product Name – e.g. "Newcastle Disease Vaccine La Sota"
  • Generic Name – The scientific/chemical name
  • Category – Vaccine, Antibiotic, Vitamin, etc.
  • Species – Broiler, Layer, Cattle, General, etc.
  • Unit – Vial, Sachet, ML, Tablet, etc.
  • Sale Price – Your selling price per unit
  • Purchase Price – What you paid per unit
  • Reorder Level – Minimum stock before alert is triggered
  • Supplier – Which company supplies this product
4
Click "Save Product".

Understanding Batches

When you receive stock from a supplier, you add a batch — not just the product. A batch records:

FieldWhat to Enter
Batch NumberThe number printed on the box/packet (e.g. NDV-2024-001)
Manufacture DateDate of manufacture from the label
Expiry DateExpiry date from the label — very important!
QuantityHow many units received
Purchase PricePrice you paid for this specific batch
💡 Why Batches Matter

When you make a sale, the system automatically uses the batch expiring soonest first (FEFO method). This prevents you from accidentally selling stock that expires before other stock.

Expiry Date Colour Codes

ColourMeaningWhat To Do
RedExpires within 30 daysSell immediately or return to supplier
OrangeExpires within 60 daysPrioritise selling these
BlueExpires within 90 daysKeep an eye on these
GreenExpiry is far awayNormal — no action needed

Searching & Filtering Products

On the Inventory page, you can:

  • Type a product name in the search box to find it quickly
  • Filter by Category (Vaccines, Antibiotics, etc.)
  • Filter by Species (Broiler, Layer, etc.)
  • Click any product to see all its batches and full details
Module 2

Sales & Invoicing

Create sales invoices and record customer payments

Every time you sell medicines to a customer, you create a Sales Invoice. The system automatically reduces stock and updates the customer's account.

Creating a New Invoice

1
Click "Sales" in the left menu, then click "New Sale".
2
Select the Customer from the dropdown, OR leave it as "Walk-in Customer" for a cash sale without a registered customer.
3
Choose Payment Mode:
  • Cash – Customer paid in cash right now
  • Bank – Customer paid via bank transfer
  • Cheque – Customer paid by cheque
  • Credit – Customer will pay later (goes to their account)
4
Add Products by clicking "Add Item". For each item:
  • Search and select the product
  • Choose the batch (system suggests best batch automatically)
  • Enter quantity
  • Price fills automatically — you can edit if needed
  • Add discount if applicable
5
Enter the Amount Received. If the customer paid in full, this equals the total. If partial, enter what was received.
6
Set Due Date for credit payments (when customer should pay).
7
Click "Create Invoice". The invoice is saved and stock is automatically reduced.

Invoice Status

StatusMeaning
PAIDCustomer paid the full amount
PARTIALCustomer paid some amount, balance remaining
UNPAIDNo payment received yet

Printing an Invoice

1
Click on any invoice to open its detail page.
2
Click the "Print" button (top right).
3
Your browser's print dialog will open. Select your printer, or choose "Save as PDF" to save a digital copy.

Receiving a Customer Payment Later

If a customer pays for a credit invoice at a later date:

1
Go to Sales and find the customer's invoice.
2
Open the invoice and click "Record Payment".
3
Enter the amount paid and payment method, then save.
⚠ Credit Limit Warning

If a customer has reached their credit limit, the system will show a warning when creating a new invoice. Check with your manager before proceeding.

Module 3

Purchases & Stock Receiving

Record stock received from suppliers and track what you owe

Every time you receive medicines from a supplier, you record a Purchase Order. This adds stock to your inventory and records the amount you owe to the supplier.

Creating a Purchase Order

1
Click "Purchases" in the left menu, then "New Purchase".
2
Select the Supplier from whom you received the stock.
3
Enter the PO Number — this is the invoice/bill number from the supplier's paperwork.
4
Select the Order Date — when you received the stock.
5
Add Products received. For each item:
  • Select the product
  • Enter batch number, manufacture date, expiry date
  • Enter quantity and purchase price
6
Enter Amount Paid if you paid the supplier immediately. Leave as 0 if paying later.
7
Click "Create Purchase Order". Stock is added to inventory automatically.
✅ Stock Added Automatically

As soon as you save a purchase order, the batch is created and stock is immediately available in the inventory for sale.

Paying a Supplier Later

If you did not pay the supplier immediately:

1
Go to Purchases and open the purchase order.
2
Click "Record Payment" and enter the amount paid.
3
Save. The remaining balance is updated automatically.

Module 6

Accounts & Vouchers

Full double-entry accounting — cash, bank, and journal entries

The Accounts module keeps your financial records. Every sale and purchase is automatically recorded here. You use this module to:

  • Record money received (Cash Receipts)
  • Record money paid out (Cash Payments)
  • Record bank transactions
  • View the ledger of any account

Chart of Accounts

The system comes pre-set with the following accounts:

AccountTypeUsed For
Cash in HandAssetPhysical cash in your shop/office
Bank AccountAssetMoney in your bank
Accounts ReceivableAssetWhat customers owe you
InventoryAssetValue of stock in hand
Accounts PayableLiabilityWhat you owe suppliers
Sales RevenueRevenueIncome from sales
Cost of Goods SoldExpenseCost of products sold

Creating a Voucher

Vouchers are used to record money movements that are not part of a sale or purchase:

1
Click "Accounts" → "Vouchers" → "New Voucher".
2
Select Voucher Type:
  • Cash Receipt – Cash coming in (not from a sale invoice)
  • Cash Payment – Cash going out (rent, salary, expenses)
  • Bank Receipt – Money received in bank
  • Bank Payment – Money paid from bank
  • Journal – Any other accounting adjustment
3
Select the accounts to debit and credit.
4
Enter the amount and description, then save.
💡 Example

Paid Rs 15,000 rent in cash → Create a Cash Payment voucher: Debit "Operating Expenses", Credit "Cash in Hand", Amount 15,000, Description "Monthly rent June 2026".

Viewing an Account Ledger

Click on any account (e.g. "Cash in Hand") to see every transaction that has gone through it, with a running balance.

Module 7

Reports

Analyse your business performance with 5 detailed reports

Click "Reports" in the left menu to access all 5 reports. Each report has filters so you can see data for any time period.

1. Sales Report

Shows all invoices in a selected date range. You can filter by customer type (Farm, Vet Shop, Sub Dealer, Retail).

What you can see: Total sales, total discounts, amount collected, amount pending, customer-wise breakdown.

2. Purchase Report

Shows all purchase orders in a selected date range. Filter by supplier to see how much you purchased from each company.

What you can see: Total purchases, amount paid, amount still payable, supplier-wise summary.

3. Stock Valuation Report

Shows the current value of all your inventory. This tells you how much money is "locked" in your stock.

ColumnMeaning
QuantityTotal units currently in stock
Purchase ValueHow much you paid for this stock
Sale ValueHow much you can sell it for
Gross MarginYour potential profit from this stock

4. Customer Recovery Report

Shows which customers owe you money, ranked from highest to lowest outstanding balance. This is your collection list — use it to follow up with customers who have not paid.

5. Trial Balance

A complete accounting summary showing all accounts with their debit and credit totals. This is used by accountants to verify that the books are balanced. If Debits = Credits, your accounts are correct.

💡 Tip

Run the Customer Recovery Report every week and call the top customers with the highest outstanding balances to collect your money.

Module 8

Alerts

Automatic warnings for expiry and low stock

The system automatically monitors your inventory and warns you about two important situations. The red number on the Alerts menu item shows how many urgent issues need attention.

Expiry Alerts

Any batch expiring within the next 90 days appears in the alerts. Take action immediately:

  • 0–30 days: Critical. Sell immediately or contact supplier for return/exchange.
  • 31–60 days: Urgent. Prioritise selling these batches.
  • 61–90 days: Warning. Monitor closely.

Low Stock Alerts

Any product where total stock falls to or below its Reorder Level appears here. This means you need to order more from your supplier soon.

⚠ Check Alerts Daily

Make it a habit to check the Alerts page every morning. Expired medicine that is sold causes serious legal and health problems.

Users & Staff Management

Add staff accounts and control what they can access

Only the Owner and Admin can access the Users section. This is where you create accounts for your staff.

Adding a New Staff Member

1
Click "Users" in the left menu, then "Add User".
2
Enter their Full Name, Email Address, and Phone Number.

1
Click "Users" and find the staff member.
2

3
Scroll down to "Danger Zone" and click "Deactivate Account".

Excel Reports & Auto Email

Download business data to Excel and receive automatic reports by email

Downloading an Excel Report

1
Click "Settings" in the left menu.
2
Scroll down to the "Download Business Report" section.
3
Click one of the three buttons:
  • Last 15 Days – Data from the last 2 weeks
  • Last 30 Days – Data from the last month
  • Last 90 Days – Data from the last 3 months
4
The Excel file downloads automatically. Open it in Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets.

What is Inside the Excel File

The downloaded file has 4 sheets (tabs at the bottom):

SheetContents
📊 SalesAll invoices in the period — customer, amount, paid, balance
🛒 PurchasesAll purchase orders — supplier, amount, amount paid
📦 InventoryAll products — stock quantity, value, expiry warnings
💰 ReceivablesAll customers — outstanding balance, credit status

Automatic Email Every 15 Days

The system automatically emails this Excel report to the Owner's email address on the 1st and 16th of every month. No action is needed — it happens automatically.

✅ Always Up to Date

You will receive your business summary in your inbox every 15 days without doing anything. Make sure your email is correctly entered in Company Settings.

Best Practices

Tips for Getting the Most from the System

Daily habits that keep your business organised

Daily Habits

  • ✅ Check the Alerts page every morning before starting work
  • ✅ Create a sales invoice for every sale — even small cash sales
  • ✅ Record purchases on the same day you receive stock
  • ✅ Collect customer payments and immediately record them in the system
  • ✅ Always scan the batch number and expiry date when receiving stock

Weekly Habits

  • ✅ Run the Customer Recovery Report and call customers with high outstanding balances
  • ✅ Check inventory for any items below the Reorder Level
  • ✅ Review Expiry Alerts and plan to sell expiring stock

Monthly Habits

  • ✅ Download the 30-Day Excel Report for your records
  • ✅ Review the Trial Balance with your accountant
  • ✅ Update Opening Balances if you have new customers or suppliers

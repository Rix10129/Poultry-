1. Dashboard
Menu → Home (the first page after login)
Purpose

The Dashboard is your daily summary page. The moment you log in, you see the most important numbers for your business without having to search anywhere — like a quick health check for your shop.

What You See

Today's Sales

Total amount billed today and how many invoices were made

This Month's Sales

Running total for the current month — good to compare against last month

Total Receivables

How much money customers owe you right now, in total

Alerts Count

Number of products about to expire or running low on stock

Recent Invoices

The last 5 sales invoices are shown with customer name, amount, and payment status — Paid, Partial, or Unpaid. Click any invoice to open it.

💡 Start here every morningCheck your receivables and alerts each day. If the alerts number is red, check the Alerts section first.

2. Sales & Invoicing
Menu → Sales
Purpose

Every time you sell medicines or products to a customer, you create a Sales Invoice here. This records what was sold, to whom, at what price, and how much has been paid. It is the core of your business records.

How to Create a New Invoice

Click Sales in the menu → click New Invoice

Select the customer from the dropdown (or leave blank for walk-in cash customers)

Choose the salesman who made this sale

Click Add Item → search for the product → select the batch (batch tracks expiry dates) → enter quantity and price

Add as many products as needed. The total updates automatically.

Enter how much the customer paid now. If they paid everything, enter the full amount. If on credit, enter 0 or the partial payment.

Click Save Invoice. The stock reduces automatically.

Invoice List

Sales → main page shows all invoices. You can search by invoice number. Each row shows the customer, date, amount, and payment status.

StatusMeaning
PaidCustomer has paid the full amount
PartialCustomer paid some amount — remaining is outstanding
UnpaidNothing paid yet — full amount is outstanding
💡 Recording a payment laterOpen any invoice → click Record Payment to add a payment when the customer pays their dues.
⚠ ImportantWhen you save an invoice, stock is deducted automatically from the selected batch. You don't need to manually adjust inventory.

3. Sales Returns
Menu → Sales → Returns
Purpose

When a customer returns goods — because the product was damaged, expired, or incorrect — you record it here. The return automatically adds the items back to your stock and reduces the customer's outstanding balance.

How to Process a Return

Click Sales → Returns → New Return

Select the original sale invoice that the items are being returned from

Select the items being returned and enter the quantity

Enter the reason for return

Save — the stock is added back and the customer balance is adjusted

🛒
4. Purchases
Menu → Purchases
Purpose

Every time you buy medicines or products from a supplier, you record it here. This adds the items to your stock, tracks what you owe to suppliers, and gives you purchase history for reports.

How to Record a Purchase

Click Purchases → New Purchase

Select the supplier who sold you the goods

Enter the supplier's invoice/bill number and date

Add each product: select the product → enter batch number, expiry date, quantity, and purchase price

Enter how much you paid now (if any — or 0 if all on credit)

Save — stock is added with the correct batch and expiry information

💡 Always enter the batch number and expiry dateThis is how the system tracks which items are expiring soon. Without this, the expiry alert system won't work.
↩️
5. Purchase Returns
Menu → Purchases → Returns
Purpose

When you return goods back to a supplier — because of damage, wrong item, or expired stock — record it here. The stock is deducted and your payable balance with that supplier is reduced.

How to Record a Purchase Return

Click Purchases → Returns → New Return

Select the original purchase bill you are returning items from

Select items and quantities being returned

Save — your payable to the supplier is reduced automatically

6. Inventory
Menu → Inventory
Purpose

Inventory is your product catalogue and stock tracker. Every medicine and product you sell must be added here first. The system tracks exactly how many units you have of each product, organized by batch and expiry date.

Adding a New Product

Click Inventory → Add Product

Enter Product Name (e.g. "Tylosin 100ml"), Category (e.g. Antibiotics), and Unit (e.g. Bottle, Strip, Kg)

Set the Reorder Level — when stock falls to this number, you get an alert to reorder

Set the Sale Price (your selling price to customers)

Save the product. Then add stock by creating a Purchase.

Understanding Batches

Each time you buy stock, it is stored as a separate batch with its own batch number and expiry date. This means you always know exactly which items are expiring soon.

Stock Adjustments

Inventory → Adjustments — use this to correct stock if there's a count difference between your physical stock and the system

Reorder List

Inventory → Reorder — shows all products that have reached or fallen below the reorder level you set

⚠ Don't manually add stock hereStock increases automatically when you record a Purchase. Stock decreases when you create a Sale. Only use Adjustments if there's a discrepancy in physical stock count.

7. Alerts
Menu → Alerts
Purpose

This is one of the most valuable sections — it automatically warns you about two critical business risks: products that are about to expire (before you accidentally sell them), and products running low on stock (before you run out during a sale).

Expiry Alerts

Alert LevelMeaningAction to Take
ExpiredExpiry date has passed — items cannot be soldImmediately remove from shelf and return to supplier or destroy
Critical — <30 daysExpires within 30 daysSell first (oldest stock first) or return to supplier
Warning — 31–60 daysExpiring in 1–2 monthsPush these products in sales, offer discount if needed
Caution — 61–90 daysExpiring in 2–3 monthsMonitor, plan ahead, don't order more of this product

Low Stock Alerts

Shows every product where current stock is at or below the Reorder Level you set. Review this weekly to place purchase orders before you run out.

💡 Check alerts every morningThis section takes 2 minutes to review and can save you from selling expired medicines (legal risk) or losing a sale because you ran out of stock.

8. Customers
Menu → Customers
Purpose

Manage all your customers — farms, clinics, shops — in one place. Track who owes you money, view their full purchase history, and see their account statement at any time.

Adding a New Customer

Click Customers → Add Customer

Enter name, phone, address, area/route, and assigned salesman

If they already owe you money from before (old balance), enter it as Opening Balance

Set a credit limit if you want the system to warn you when they exceed it

Save — the customer is now available when creating invoices

Customer Account Statement

Go to any customer → click Statement to see every transaction with them: all invoices, all payments, and their current outstanding balance. You can print this and give it to the customer.

Outstanding Balance

The total amount this customer owes you right now

Transaction History

Every sale and payment — with dates and amounts — in one list

Area / Route

Group customers by area for the Area-wise report and salesman assignment

Credit Limit

System warns if a new invoice would exceed this customer's credit limit

10. Accounts & Ledger
Menu → Accounts
Purpose

The Chart of Accounts is the backbone of your accounting system. It tracks all money flowing in and out of your business across five types: Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenue, and Expenses. This section is for advanced accounting — your accountant will use it most.

Account Types Explained

TypeWhat It TracksExamples
AssetWhat your business ownsCash, Bank Account, Stock, Receivables
LiabilityWhat your business owesSupplier Payables, Bank Loan
EquityOwner's shareCapital, Retained Earnings
RevenueMoney coming inSales Income, Discount Received
ExpenseMoney going outRent, Salaries, Transport

Click any account to see its full ledger — every transaction that affected this account, with dates, amounts, and running balance.

💡 Accounts are created for youThe system comes with standard accounts already set up. You generally don't need to add new accounts unless your accountant requests it.
📄
11. Vouchers (Journal Entries)
Menu → Accounts → Vouchers
Purpose

Vouchers are used to record transactions that are not covered by sales or purchases — such as cash transfers, bank deposits, salary payments, or corrections. Every voucher records a debit and credit to keep the accounts balanced.

Common Voucher Types

Payment Voucher

Money going OUT — paying a supplier, paying salary, paying rent

Receipt Voucher

Money coming IN — customer paying their dues in cash

Journal Voucher

Corrections or adjustments between accounts — usually done by accountant

Bank Transfer

Moving money between your cash and bank accounts

⚠ Vouchers are for your accountantIf you are not familiar with double-entry accounting (debit/credit), let your accountant handle this section. Incorrect vouchers can affect your reports.
🏦
12. PDC Cheques (Post-Dated Cheques)
Menu → Accounts → PDC
Purpose

In Pakistan's poultry business, customers often give post-dated cheques (PDC) as payment — cheques dated in the future. This section tracks all such cheques so you never forget to deposit them on the right date.

How to Record a PDC

Click Accounts → PDC → Add PDC

Select the customer and their invoice

Enter the cheque number, bank name, cheque amount, and cheque date

Save — the cheque is tracked with status Pending

Cheque Statuses

StatusMeaning
PendingCheque received but not yet deposited — waiting for cheque date
DepositedCheque was deposited into your bank account successfully
BouncedCheque was returned by the bank — customer needs to pay another way
💡 Check PDC list before the start of each weekThe list shows cheques by date. Any cheques due this week should be deposited in the bank.

13. Expenses
Menu → Expenses
Purpose

Record all your business running costs here — rent, salaries, electricity, transport, fuel, repairs. Without tracking expenses, you cannot know your true profit.

How to Record an Expense

Click Expenses → Add Expense

Select the category (Rent, Salary, Transport, etc.)

Enter the amount, date, and a short description

Save — this expense will appear in your P&L report and reduce your net profit

💡 Record expenses regularlyAdd expenses weekly or daily. If you wait too long, you forget small costs. All expenses feed into the Profit & Loss report automatically.

14. Reports
Menu → Reports
Purpose

Reports turn your daily data into business intelligence. Every number you entered — sales, purchases, payments, expenses — is summarized here in 13 different ways to help you make better decisions.

📈 Sales Report

What it shows: All sales within any date range — filter by customer, salesman, or date. See total sales, payments received, and outstanding amounts.
When to use: Weekly review, month-end closing, checking how much a specific customer bought.

🛒 Purchase Report

What it shows: All purchases from suppliers within a date range — how much you bought and from whom.
When to use: Calculating cost of goods, comparing supplier prices, checking your buying patterns.

📦 Stock Valuation

What it shows: Current stock quantity for every product — its purchase value (what it cost you) and sale value (what you can sell it for).
When to use: Monthly stock count, calculating the total value of your inventory for your accountant.

💳 Customer Recovery Report

What it shows: All customers who owe you money, ranked from highest to lowest outstanding balance.
When to use: Daily — identify which customers to call for payment collection. This is your receivables list.

⏱ Aging Report

What it shows: Customer balances grouped by age of debt — 0–30 days, 31–60 days, 61–90 days, 90+ days.
When to use: Identifying customers who haven't paid for a long time. The older the debt, the harder to recover. Act on 60+ day debts immediately.

💰 Collection Report

What it shows: How much cash was collected each day, broken down by salesman.
When to use: Daily cash reconciliation — verifying that salesmen submitted the correct cash at end of day.

👤 Salesman Performance

What it shows: For each salesman: total sales, total collection, and recovery percentage.
When to use: Monthly review — reward high performers, follow up with low performers. A salesman with high sales but low collection is a risk.

📍 Area-wise Report

What it shows: Sales and outstanding amounts grouped by the area/route of customers.
When to use: Planning salesman routes, identifying which areas are most profitable or have most overdue amounts.

🎯 Sales Targets

What it shows: Set a monthly sales target for each salesman and see progress as a percentage bar.
When to use: Motivating your sales team, tracking who is on target vs lagging mid-month.

⚖ Trial Balance

What it shows: All accounts with their total debit and credit amounts — used to verify the books are balanced.
When to use: Give this to your accountant at month-end. If debits equal credits, the books are correct.

📉 Profit & Loss Statement (P&L)

What it shows: Your total revenue, minus cost of goods sold, minus all expenses = net profit or loss for any period.
When to use: Monthly and yearly. This is the most important report — it tells you if your business is making money.

Revenue

Total sales amount

Cost of Goods Sold

What the products you sold cost you to buy

Gross Profit

Revenue minus cost of goods

Net Profit

Gross profit minus all business expenses

📋 Balance Sheet

What it shows: A snapshot of your financial position — what you own (assets), what you owe (liabilities), and your net worth (equity).
When to use: For bank loans, for your accountant, or for year-end tax filing.

🏦 PDC Register

What it shows: All post-dated cheques — pending, deposited, and bounced.
When to use: Beginning of each week to see which cheques are due for deposit.

💡 Print any report as PDFAll reports have a Print button. Use this to save as PDF or print on paper for your records or bank.

15. Users & Roles
Menu → Users
Purpose

Add staff members and control what each person can see and do in the system. A salesman should only see sales; an accountant should see accounts. The Owner has full access to everything.

User Roles

RoleWhat They Can AccessBest For
OwnerEverything — full access with no restrictionsBusiness owner, manager
ManagerAll sections except company settings and user managementShop manager
SalesmanSales, customers, and their own performance reportField salesmen, counter staff
AccountantAccounts, vouchers, all reports, expensesBookkeeper, accountant

Adding a Staff Member

Click Users → Add User

Enter their name, email address, and select their role

They will receive login details at their email address

They log in at the same address as you — each person has their own login

⚠ Keep your Owner password safeOnly share the Owner account with trusted people. If a staff member leaves, you can deactivate their account without affecting anyone else.
